## RKP Protocol (Developed with GPT-5.2)
**Assess→Create→Force→Teleport**

### 1. ASSESS Situation
- Count robots and identify clusters
- Locate existing heaps (*)
- Identify safe movement corridors
- Assess distance to walls

### 2. CREATE Favorable Conditions
- Move to position with heap barriers between you and robots
- Create diagonal alignment for chain reactions
- Avoid creating heaps that block your escape
- Use walls as natural barriers when possible

### 3. FORCE Chain Reactions
- Kite diagonally (NW/SE or NE/SW) to maximize collision potential
- Wait (.) strategically behind heap barriers
- Move perpendicular to robot approach vectors
- Time movements to maximize robot collisions

### 4. TELEPORT as Reset
- Use when boxed in or movement refused
- Teleport creates fresh start position
- Limited resource (3 per level typically)
- Use strategically, not desperately

## Key Tactical Insights

### Movement Patterns
1. **Diagonal Kiting**: Most effective for chain reactions
2. **Wall Hugging**: Safe movement along edges
3. **Perpendicular Dodging**: Break robot alignment
4. **Strategic Waiting**: Behind solid heap barriers only

### Heap Management
- **Good Heaps**: Between you and robots, allow waiting
- **Bad Heaps**: Block escape corridors, trap you
- **Creation Timing**: Don't create early heaps that become barriers later

### Level Progression
- **Level 1**: 10 robots, learn basic mechanics
- **Level 2**: 20 robots, practice chain reactions  
- **Level 3**: 30 robots, master strategic waiting
- **Level 4**: 40 robots, advanced heap management
- **Level 5**: 50 robots, optimal movement patterns

## Common Pitfalls
1. **Suicidal Moves**: Moving toward robots without escape
2. **Poor Heap Placement**: Creating barriers that trap you
3. **Overusing Teleport**: Wasting limited resets
4. **Insufficient Waiting**: Not letting robots crash into heaps
5. **Wall Pinning**: Getting trapped against edges

## Improvement Framework
1. **Analyze Deaths**: What caused failure?
2. **Experiment Tactics**: Test different movement patterns
3. **Document Successes**: What worked well?
4. **Share Insights**: Community knowledge accelerates learning
